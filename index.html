Top Reasons Photos Get Rejected

Avoid these mistakes. Our tool automatically fixes the first 3.

📅

Missing Date of Photo

SSC portals use automated scanners. If DOP is missing or unclear, the application is auto-rejected.

📦

Wrong File Size

The portal strictly enforces 20–50 KB. Even 51 KB causes an upload error. Our slider gives you exact control.

🔤

Low Contrast Text

Black text on dark shirt is unreadable by scanners. Our white strip ensures maximum contrast always.

📐

Incorrect Dimensions

SSC requires 275×354 px. Stretched or squashed photos are rejected. Use our preset for exact sizing.

🗓️

DOB Instead of DOP

A very common mistake. DOB is your birthday. DOP is when the photo was taken. Always use DOP.

👓

Glasses / Cap in Photo

SSC strictly prohibits spectacles, caps, or dark glasses. Retake your photo if these are visible.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about SSC photo requirements

What is the exact photo size for SSC CGL 2026? ▼
For SSC CGL 2026, the photograph must be 275 pixels wide × 354 pixels tall (approximately 3.5cm × 4.5cm). The file must be in JPG/JPEG format and strictly between 20 KB and 50 KB. Our tool includes a dedicated SSC CGL preset that automatically outputs at these exact dimensions.
Is it mandatory to write Date of Photo (DOP) on SSC photos? ▼
Yes, traditionally SSC requires the Date of Photo (DOP) to be printed on the photograph. However, the newer SSC OTR portal uses live webcam capture for some exams. Always check your specific exam notification. If DOP is required, our tool places it on a white strip at the bottom in the DD-MM-YYYY format SSC specifies.
Is my photo safe? Does it get uploaded anywhere? ▼
100% safe. This tool uses the HTML5 Canvas API — everything runs inside your own browser. Your photo is never sent to any server, never stored in any database. When you close the tab, everything is gone. We have zero access to your images. This is verifiable — right-click anywhere and inspect network traffic; you'll see zero image uploads.
Should I write DOB or DOP on the photo? ▼
Always write DOP — Date of Photograph — which is the date when you physically took the photo. Do NOT write your Date of Birth (DOB). SSC requires recent photos (not older than 3 months from exam notification date), so printing DOP helps them verify the photo's recency. Writing DOB is one of the most common reasons for form rejection.
How do I get exactly 40KB file size? ▼
After clicking "Generate", the quality slider becomes active. Move it left to reduce file size (lower quality) or right for better quality (larger size). The live KB counter updates in real-time — aim for 25–45 KB to stay safely within the 20–50 KB range. Then click Download.
Can I use this for UPSC, Railway, Banking exams too? ▼
Yes! This tool works for all major Indian government exams: UPSC Civil Services (IAS/IPS/IFS), RRB Railway Group D/ALP/NTPC, IBPS PO/Clerk, SBI PO/Clerk, BPSC, WBCS, UP Police, DSSSB, and more. The white-strip DOP format is accepted across all Indian government portals. Use the "Original Size" preset if your exam has different pixel requirements.
