Government exams like SSC CGL, CHSL, MTS, and GD Constable have very strict rules regarding the photograph. According to the latest notifications, if a candidate's photo does not clearly mention the Date of Photo (DOP), the application is liable to be rejected instantly.

Why do you need to add Name and Date on Photo?

The primary reason for adding the Date of Photo is to ensure that the photograph is recent (not older than 3 months from the date of notification). SSC and UPSC commissions use this to verify the current appearance of the candidate. Our tool helps you add this information without cropping your face, ensuring that the proportions of your passport photo remain perfect.

Common Reasons for SSC Photo Rejection

Aspirants often lose their chance because of minor mistakes. Avoid these errors:

  • Blurred DOP: The date must be printed in black on a white background strip.
  • Spectacles & Caps: Photos with sunglasses, spectacles, or caps are rejected.
  • Improper Background: Use a light-colored, preferably white, background.
  • Old Photos: Never use a photo older than three months.
